• Go is a programming language design started in 2007 and developed as open source in Google
    by Ken Thompson, Rob Pike and Robert Griesemer in 2009. Version 1.0 was released by Google in 2012
    and as of early 2018 it had reached version 1.9.3
  • In the world of computer science, both Ken Thompson and Rob Pikes had been highly renowned
    for their works at the prestigious Bell Labs, before joining Google. Ken Thompson designed
    the original Unix operating system and created B, the predecessor to the present day legendary
    C pogramming language. Rob Pike was the member of the Bell Labs' Unix team. He and Ken Thompson
    collaborated to create the original design of UTF-8 encoding as defined by Unicode standards.
    Their UTF-8 encoding system is backward-compatible with ASCII and has been dominant in character
    encoding for World Wide Web since 2009.
  • The three inventors of Golang mentioned their shared dislikes for the overly complicated C++ as one
    of the main reason prompting them to design the new programming language.

  • Go is also known as Golang and according to its official website at Golang.org,
    "Go is a general-purpose language designed with systems programming in mind. It is strongly typed and
    garbage-collected and has explicit support for concurrent programming."
    source https://golang.org/ref/spec
  • Balbaert(2012) mentioned that Golang was created for ease of programming, fast compilation and execution.
    Balbaert, I.: The way to Go: A thorough introduction to the Go programming language. iUniverse, USA (2012)
  • From my understanding, the language offers a concise alternative to object orientation methodology
    and yet, it presents a powerful approach to program scalable and natively concurrent codes.
    This was designed to harness the power of parallel processing enabled by multicore CPUs which
    first generations of were available to the general public back in 2005/2006.
    Probably, the inventors wanted to create a language which is modern and powerful, but at the same time
    more streamlined and less complexed than C++. Created shortly after the first availabilty of multicore
    processing, they must had designed Golang with concurrency in mind. Due to this, in comparison to
    the older, pre-multicore languages, Golang is claimed to able to do a lot more multiple things at any
    one time. This capability in handling outstanding processing volume drives up the scalability of the
    language.
