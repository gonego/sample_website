• Go is a programming language design started in 2007 and developed as open source in Google by Ken Thompson, Rob Pike and Robert Griesemer in 2009. Version 1.0 was released by Google in 2012 and as of early 2018 it had reached version 1.9.3 • In the world of computer science, both Ken Thompson and Rob Pikes had been highly renowned for their works at the prestigious Bell Labs, before joining Google. Ken Thompson designed the original Unix operating system and created B, the predecessor to the present day legendary C pogramming language. Rob Pike was the member of the Bell Labs' Unix team. He and Ken Thompson collaborated to create the original design of UTF-8 encoding as defined by Unicode standards. Their UTF-8 encoding system is backward-compatible with ASCII and has been dominant in character encoding for World Wide Web since 2009.
